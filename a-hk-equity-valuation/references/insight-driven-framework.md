# Insight-Driven Framework

Use this framework when the user wants the company's excess returns explained through Insight: a critical cognitive edge, structural advantage, or business truth that produced superior economics.

Be strict. If you cannot identify a real and currently effective Insight, say so explicitly. Do not elevate a weak story into an Insight.

Treat Insight like an ice block that was pushed onto a surface. The initial push created motion, but friction and melting always work against it. Your task is to identify what the push was, whether the block is still moving, and how much useful glide remains.

## Required Sections

## 1. Historical Insight Archaeology

Trace the company's development and identify its past core Insights.

For each candidate Insight, answer:

- What exactly was the Insight in one sentence?
- Was it actively discovered by management or passively enjoyed as a structural or era-specific advantage?
- During what period did it matter most?
- What excess economics did it produce?
- Why did it weaken or fail: imitation, technology shift, regulation change, consumer change, capital cycle reversal, or loss of execution?

Do not assume an old Insight is still alive.

## 2. Current Insight Scan

This is the center of gravity.

Test the main possible sources of current Insight:

- Rare managerial cognition or operating method
- Hard-to-copy structural position: network effects, brand mindshare, licenses, ecosystem lock-in, supply-chain control, distribution choke point
- Business-type superiority: a category with unusually attractive economics compared with ordinary peers

For each candidate, decide whether it truly exists today.

If one exists, state its core in one sentence.
If none survives close inspection, say `No valid Insight identified`.

## 3. Ice-Block State Assessment

For each currently valid Insight, assess its state using the sliding ice-block model.

Evaluate:

- Current speed: are the excess returns accelerating, stable, or decelerating?
- Melting: are the underlying conditions supporting the Insight weakening?
- Friction: how strong are competition, regulation, substitution, or execution drag?
- Remaining glide: how far can this Insight plausibly continue to matter?

Choose one state for each Insight:

- Far from slowing
- Still gliding but clearly slowing
- Near stall
- Already stopped

## 4. False-Insight Detection

Identify the common bullish narratives that do not qualify as real Insight.

Examples may include:

- Scale without durable economics
- Brand fame without pricing power
- Historical success carried forward mechanically
- Temporary scarcity mistaken for structural advantage
- Management charisma mistaken for repeatable method

Explain why each narrative fails the Insight test.

## 5. Investor Conclusion

End with one sentence answering:

- From the perspective of Insight, is this company worth following or owning for the long term?

The reason should point to the presence, absence, durability, or decay of real Insight.

## Integration With Valuation And Other Lenses

When used alongside valuation:

- A live Insight can justify stronger confidence in duration, margins, reinvestment, and multiple support.
- A fading Insight should reduce confidence, shorten forecast horizons, and widen downside scenarios.
- If valuation appears cheap but Insight has already stalled, explain that the apparent discount may be a fair reflection of deteriorating economics rather than an opportunity.
