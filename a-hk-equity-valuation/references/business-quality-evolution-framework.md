# Business Quality Evolution Framework

Use this framework when the user wants to understand how the intrinsic quality of a company's core business changed across a long time span, rather than whether recent operating performance improved or weakened.

The central question is whether the same business became a different kind of business in terms of barrier quality, value-chain position, pricing power, strategic weight, demand structure, and replaceability.

Write in connected paragraphs. Organize around moments of change, not a year-by-year chronology. If a fact cannot be verified, mark it as `To be verified`.

## Core Lenses

Follow two lines of analysis and then bring them together.

## 1. Endogenous Evolution

Track how the company's own capability accumulation changed the nature of the business.

Focus on:

- Capability-boundary migration
- Value-chain position migration
- Pricing-power change
- Customer-relationship change
- Any move from replaceable participant to bottleneck participant

Identify the key transition nodes:

- In what period did the company cross an important capability threshold?
- What event, investment, learning curve, or accumulation made the change durable?
- Why was the change hard to reverse?

## 2. Exogenous Evolution

Track how broader industry change redefined the strategic weight of the business itself.

Focus on:

- Structural expansion of demand
- Barrier escalation on the supply side
- Shift in power across the value chain
- Change in substitutability versus necessity
- Re-rating of the business within the larger economic system

Identify the key redefinition nodes:

- In what period did the business become more central or more dispensable?
- Which industry changes altered its strategic importance?
- Did the business move from side role to choke point, or from essential input to commoditized layer?

## 3. Convergence Of The Two Lines

The two lines should not remain separate.

Explain:

- Whether internal accumulation matched an external window
- Whether external change amplified or wasted prior internal effort
- Whether management anticipated the shift or mainly benefited after the fact

## 4. Start-End Contrast

End with a sharp contrast between the start year and the end year.

Compare in plain language:

- Barrier strength
- Customer bargaining relationship
- Replaceability
- Strategic weight in the industry
- What the business fundamentally was at the start versus at the end

The goal is to show how the same business label may hide two very different economic qualities.

## Output Expectations

- Use Markdown
- Prefer connected analysis over bullet-heavy formatting
- Concentrate on true transition stages and pivotal nodes
- Avoid dramatic storytelling; favor mechanism and factual support
- Mark uncertain claims as `To be verified`
