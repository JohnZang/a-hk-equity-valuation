# Entropy External-Change Framework

Use this framework when the user wants to know whether a company has already encountered a major external environmental change that can break the ordered system supporting its prosperity.

Start from one belief: excellent companies create local order in a disorderly world, but order can be broken when the external environment changes at the ecological level rather than through ordinary noise.

## Core Question

Has a major external environmental change already arrived?

Do not confuse normal turbulence with structural change. Routine earnings volatility, management turnover, or small policy adjustments are not enough.

## Six Dimensions

For each dimension, assign one label:

- `No such threat`
- `Questionable threat`
- `Confirmed threat`

## 1. Technological Substitution

Check whether a new technology can fundamentally replace the company's core product or service rather than merely improve competition at the margin.

## 2. Demand Disappearance

Check whether the underlying need the company serves is shrinking, being redefined, or migrating elsewhere.

## 3. Regulatory Rewrite

Check whether policy or regulation is changing industry rules deeply enough to alter profit structure, access, or business legitimacy.

## 4. Ecosystem Breakdown

Check whether the supply chain, platform layer, partner network, or broader business ecology the company depends on is destabilizing.

## 5. Competitive Leap

Check whether a different kind of competitor has appeared, making the company's prior advantage irrelevant rather than merely contested.

## 6. Resource Fracture

Check whether a key resource such as talent, capital, data, distribution access, or natural input is becoming structurally harder to secure.

## Judgment Principles

- Apply asymmetric caution. If evidence is incomplete but a structural threat is plausible, keep it as `Questionable threat`.
- Focus on ecology-level forces, not temporary storms.
- Be willing to disagree with market consensus if the large external risk is visible but underappreciated.
- Use public evidence and say clearly when a dimension lacks enough information.

## Output Format

Use this exact structure when the user asks for this lens:

# [Company Name]: Major External Environmental Change Assessment

## Company Overview

Use two to three sentences to summarize the core business and current competitive position.

## Six-Dimension Review

For each of the six dimensions, write two to five sentences and assign one label:

- `No such threat`
- `Questionable threat`
- `Confirmed threat`

### 1. Technological Substitution
### 2. Demand Disappearance
### 3. Regulatory Rewrite
### 4. Ecosystem Breakdown
### 5. Competitive Leap
### 6. Resource Fracture

## Overall Judgment

Choose one:

- `No major external environmental change yet`
- `Warning signals have appeared`
- `Major external environmental change has arrived`

Then add one or two paragraphs explaining the logic behind the judgment in a cool, restrained tone.
