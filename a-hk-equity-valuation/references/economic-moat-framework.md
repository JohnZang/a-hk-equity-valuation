# Economic Moat Framework

Use this framework when the user wants a strict assessment of whether a company has a real and durable economic moat.

Adopt a skeptical stance. Most companies that look strong do not truly possess a moat. False moat recognition is more dangerous than no moat because it encourages unjustified confidence.

## Core Rule

Try to falsify each moat source before accepting it. If evidence is weak, say the moat is uncertain or absent.

## Five Sources Of Moat

## 1. Intangible Assets

Check for:

- Brand that supports sustained price premium
- Patents, trade secrets, or proprietary know-how with real exclusion power
- Regulatory licenses or franchises that restrict entry

Do not confuse fame with a brand moat. The real test is whether customers keep paying more and are hard to persuade away.

Judgment choices:

- Exists and strong
- Exists but fragile
- Does not exist

## 2. Switching Costs

Check for:

- Deep workflow embedding
- Migration cost in money, time, risk, retraining, or integration effort
- Customer behavior after contracts end

Do not confuse temporary contracts with true switching costs.

Judgment choices:

- Exists and strong
- Exists but fragile
- Does not exist

## 3. Network Effects

Check for:

- Whether additional users directly improve value for existing users
- Whether the effect is global, local, or only partial
- Whether the network can fragment or multi-home easily

Do not confuse large user count with a network effect.

Judgment choices:

- Exists and strong
- Exists but fragile
- Does not exist

## 4. Cost Advantages

Check for:

- Structural cost edge from scale, location, process, resource access, or route density
- Whether peers can realistically copy the cost structure
- Whether the advantage survives across cycles rather than only during a favorable period

Do not confuse low margin with cost advantage.

Judgment choices:

- Exists and strong
- Exists but fragile
- Does not exist

## 5. Efficient Scale

Check for:

- Whether the market is small or specialized enough that new entry would destroy industry returns
- Whether current players already satisfy demand
- Whether profits are attractive enough to invite entrants anyway

Do not confuse high market share with efficient scale.

Judgment choices:

- Exists and strong
- Exists but fragile
- Does not exist

## Evidence Standards

Every dimension should rely on observable evidence where possible, including:

- Long-run ROIC or ROE versus cost of capital
- Margin level and stability versus peers
- Market-share stability or drift over five to ten years
- Price realization and pricing power history
- Customer retention or renewal behavior
- Competitive response and ease of imitation

Compare both against close competitors and against the company's own history.

## Output Format

Use this exact structure when the user asks for a moat-specific report:

# [Company Name]: Economic Moat Assessment

## Company Overview

Summarize the core business, industry position, and economic model in two to three sentences.

## Five-Dimension Moat Review

For each of the five dimensions, write three to six sentences and assign one label:

- `Exists and strong`
- `Exists but fragile`
- `Does not exist`

### 1. Intangible Assets
### 2. Switching Costs
### 3. Network Effects
### 4. Cost Advantages
### 5. Efficient Scale

## Overall Judgment

Choose one:

- `Wide Moat`
- `Narrow Moat`
- `Uncertain`
- `No Moat`

Then add one or two concluding paragraphs explaining:

- Which moat dimension matters most
- Which risk worries you most
- Your candid five-to-ten-year durability view
