# Competitive Divergence Framework

Use this framework when the user wants to understand how a group of highly comparable companies started from similar positions and ended up with materially different outcomes over time.

The central question is not who won in hindsight, but how comparable firms encountered the same environment and still diverged through different choices, capabilities, timing, and luck.

Write in connected paragraphs. Organize around divergence itself, not around separate mini-histories for each company. If a fact or number cannot be confirmed, mark it as `To be verified`.

## 1. Starting-Line Calibration

First establish whether the companies were truly comparable at the starting point.

Cover:

- Business scope
- Revenue scale
- Market position
- Resource endowment
- Shared industry backdrop and opportunity window

If the starting positions were only superficially similar, say so. The divergence analysis is only meaningful when the baseline is credible.

## 2. Divergence Process

Identify the main periods when trajectories visibly split.

For each major divergence stage, compare the firms through these dimensions:

- Strategic trade-offs: focus versus diversification, core capability building versus short-term opportunity chasing
- Capital discipline: leverage, capex discipline, cash-flow control, expansion pace
- Sensing and response to industry change: whether the firms noticed technological, demand, or structural change, how early they noticed, and whether their response was correct and timely
- Management cognition and decision-making: how leadership understood the business, the moat, and customer value; whether public statements or decisions reveal the gap; whether key leadership changes altered direction
- Execution and organizational quality: product, operations, organizational energy, consistency of follow-through

## 3. End-Point Contrast

At the end date, compare the companies directly on:

- Revenue
- Profitability
- Market value where relevant
- Market share where relevant
- Competitive position
- Business quality

Quantify the scale of separation where possible.

## 4. Attribution

Conclude with a clear explanation of the deepest causes of divergence.

Address:

- What one or two differences mattered most
- Whether the decisive gap came from a single turning point or long accumulation
- Whether some decisions looked reasonable at the time but later proved fatal
- How much of the divergence came from managerial choice versus external luck or timing

## Analysis Discipline

- Keep the tone fair to both winners and losers.
- Do not assume every winner move was brilliant or every loser move was foolish.
- If all firms were mediocre and the outcome differences are mostly relative, say so directly.
- Guard against hindsight bias by reconstructing what was knowable at the time.
- Mark uncertain facts as `To be verified`.

## Output Expectations

- Use Markdown
- Prefer connected comparison over separate company writeups
- Allocate most space to the phases and dimensions where divergence widened fastest
- Keep mechanism and factual support ahead of narrative flourish
